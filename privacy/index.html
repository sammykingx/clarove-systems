Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect personal data in connection with our operational frameworks.

1. Who We Are

Clarove Systems Limited ("Clarove Systems", "we", "us", "our") is a software development company incorporated in the Federal Republic of Nigeria under the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA). We operate entirely as a digital-first business with no physical branch offices outside Nigeria. We provide software development services and related digital products to clients and businesses globally.

// Core Offerings:

Custom website, web application, and mobile app development (iOS & Android).

Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), marketplace, and e-commerce platform building.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP) architectures.

Web hosting, domain registration, and managed business email ecosystems.

Software maintenance, support, and scheduling lifecycle systems.

2. Our Commitment to Privacy

As a Nigerian-incorporated entity, our primary compliance data engine operates under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) and the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation 2019 (NDPR).

Because we orchestrate digital frameworks globally, we align our structures to support equivalent standards protective of your rights across the UK GDPR, EU GDPR, Canadian PIPEDA, and United States frameworks including the CCPA/CPRA. Where architectural variations apply, we enforce the layer offering the maximum protective standard.

3. Scope of This Policy

This Policy governs prospective clients, engaged client administrators, end-users interacting with software maintained by us on behalf of our clients, and individuals using ancillary infrastructure services.

// Controller vs. Processor Node Distinction

Data Controller: When you engage us directly as a client, Clarove Systems regulates your operational setup records.

Data Processor: When we host or manage an application for you, we handle your end-user data strictly under your instruction. In this mode, you act as the Controller, and our relationship is bound by an explicit Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

4. What Personal Data We Collect

We log minimal transactional data fields essential to engineering system endpoints:

4.1 Identity and Contact Data

Full name, company name, business email, contact phone, and geopolitical region during onboarding.

4.2 Project and Requirements Data

Specifications, assets, written copy, API credentials, or tokens shared to facilitate system engineering.

4.3 Contractual and Correspondence Data

Email logs, Statements of Work, support tickets. Note: We do not log, record, or store telephone or video streams; context is saved exclusively via written email follow-ups.

4.4 Billing and Transaction Data

Invoicing details and tokenized references. Raw payment instruments are fully isolated; transaction routing relies on PCI-DSS compliant third-party nodes (Paystack, Flutterwave, Stripe).

4.5 Hosting and Managed Services Data

Isolated data stored inside managed cloud components, accessed purely for infrastructure upkeep.

4.6 Website Usage Data

Technical logging including IP address, browser metadata strings, and analytics events to secure node performance.

5. How We Collect Your Data

Telemetry and asset collection follow established pipelines:

  • Direct Submission: Input forms, contract execution layers, or inbound project inquiries.
  • Ongoing Project Streams: System tickets, shared repositories, and architectural assets.
  • Automated Telemetry: Server analytics and tracking arrays when executing page requests.
  • Authorized Access: Direct system keys provisioned to us by clients for active engineering deployment.

Processing operations execute solely under recognized legal bases aligned across NDPA and GDPR rule frameworks:

7. How We Use Your Personal Data

Operational data structures are restricted exclusively to:

  • Formulating proposals, agreement documents, and setting up workflows.
  • Designing, executing code deployment, and provisioning software configurations.
  • Managing account maintenance, service billing cycles, and system renewals.
  • Mitigating threat profiles, protecting network nodes, and verifying code integrity.
  • Meeting international regulatory reporting or mandatory legal demands.

8. What We Do Not Do

We never sell or rent consumer identity coordinates to data syndicates.

We never record or process call logs or stream conversations natively.

We never index or hold plain text card instruments or access pins.

We never feed customer code assets or personal data vectors into public AI training modules.

9. Sharing Your Personal Data

Data sharing pathways are confined to validated operations:

  • Sub-processors: Cloud nodes (AWS, Google Cloud, DigitalOcean, Hetzner), DNS managers, registries, accounting packages, and payment processors bounded under strict data processing mandates.
  • Client Instruction: Custom integrations with external components explicitly specified inside project requirements.
  • Regulatory Mandate: Submissions required by the NDPC, judicial order, or law enforcement entities when governed under legal frameworks.

10. International Data Transfers

Clarove Systems is based in Nigeria, utilizing sub-processor hardware distributed internationally across UK, EU, and global cloud nodes.

Cross-border transfers adhere strictly to Section 43 of the NDPA 2023 and NDPR Article 2.11 requirements. We implement Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and verify that recipient clusters provide legal protection mechanisms equivalent to domestic mandates.

11. Data Retention Lifecycle

We preserve personal metrics only for durations dictated by compliance and operations:

// Contracts & Agreements 7 Years post-termination
// Project Email Logs 7 Years after project close
// Financial Documents 7 Years (CAMA / FIRS rule)
// Hosted Application Spaces Duration of service (30-day purge cycle)

12. Cookies and Website Tracking

Our page systems utilize cookies to organize runtime sessions. Strictly necessary tracking variants compile base interface features. Performance or marketing variants require affirmative visitor authorization via our interactive consent banner framework.

Canada (PIPEDA)

Data visibility requests, deletion access paths, and non-discrimination mechanisms for all covered states.

14. Security Frameworks

We deploy standard technological security shields, including mandatory TLS/HTTPS payload encryption, fine-grained access control tables, and secret management tools.

In case of an anomaly affecting system layers, we commit to notifying the Nigeria Data Protection Commission within a 72-hour timeline from identification, as dictated by NDPA protocols.

15. Data Processing Agreements

When we process data strictly as a processor on behalf of clients, a detailed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) must be put in place before processing begins. To invoke a custom DPA framework, dispatch your parameters directly to our data security mailbox.

16. Marketing Communications

We provide periodic ecosystem updates or technical service announcements. You can opt out of marketing communications at any point by selecting the "Unsubscribe" action embedded in our communications or by updating your choices via email.

17. Children's Privacy

Our software frameworks target adult operators and business entities. We do not index profiles belonging to individuals under 18 years of age. If data concerning minors is found to have been collected inadvertently, notify us immediately for swift removal.

18. Changes to This Policy

We adjust this policy framework periodically to keep pace with changing operational steps and legal mandates. Material structural re-alignments will be broadcast directly via email updates to current system contacts.
