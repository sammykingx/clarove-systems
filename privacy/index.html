Privacy Policy

At Clarove Systems, we take your privacy seriously. This policy explains exactly how we collect, protect, and verify your data whilst interacting with our offerings.

1. Overview

Clarove Systems ("we," "our," or "us") is dedicated to architecting and maintaining secure systems that respect your privacy. This Privacy Policy governs data collection, validation, and usage protocol across our websites, hosted applications, and professional client engagement frameworks.

As an engineering agency operating cross-border, our processing infrastructures are built for native compliance with both the United Kingdom General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA).

2. Information We Collect

We collect minimal personal data essential to providing premium architecture, design, and development services. This falls into two validated telemetry categories:

  • Identity & Communication Data: Name, business email, phone number, company profile, and country of origin provided voluntarily during consultation.
  • Technical Analytics: Anonymized data including IP addresses, browser types, and usage patterns collected via cookies to optimize system integrity and site performance.

3. Legal Basis & How We Use Data

We process personal data only when authorized under applicable global laws. This occurs primarily under the following conditions within our operational framework:

Contractual Necessity: To coordinate service agreements, hand off digital products, and manage technical workflows.

Legal Obligations: To verify financial transactions and maintain operational accounting records.

Legitimate Interests: To secure our digital architecture, prevent platform abuse, and measure user experience engagement.

4. Cross-Border Transfers & Storage

Clarove Systems operates via dual nodes in London, UK and Lagos, Nigeria. Data may be transferred between cloud nodes hosted on secure, encrypted infrastructure located within the UK, EU, or approved global data centers.

We implement Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and data transfer agreements to guarantee that your information retains an equivalent level of security regardless of geographic boundaries.

5. Your Global Data Rights

Depending on your location, you hold protected rights regarding your personal information under the UK GDPR or NDPR. These include your rights to:

Access & Portability

Request a full, digital copy of any personal data we hold or process on your behalf.

Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

Request the permanent destruction or deletion of your specific personal contact records.

Rectification

Correct inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated details within your active operational file.

Restriction

Object to or halt the automated processing of analytics records regarding your traffic.
