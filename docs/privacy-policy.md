## 2. Our Commitment to Privacy

As a Nigerian-incorporated entity, our primary data protection obligation is to comply with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) and the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation 2019 (NDPR), administered by the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC).

Because we offer services to individuals and businesses globally, we also align our practices with:

- The UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the UK Data Protection Act 2018, for data subjects in the United Kingdom
- The EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU GDPR), for data subjects in EU/EEA member states
- The Canadian Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), for data subjects in Canada
- Applicable United States privacy laws including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), for California residents

Where these frameworks impose different obligations, we apply the standard that is most protective of your rights.

## 3. Scope of This Policy

This Policy applies to:

- Prospective clients who visit our website or contact us to enquire about our services
- Clients and their authorised contacts who engage us to build or manage software
- End-users of software applications built, hosted, or managed by Clarové Systems on behalf of a client
- Individuals who purchase or use ancillary services we provide or manage, including hosting, domain registration, business email, and booking software

**Important distinction — controller vs. processor:**
When you engage us directly as a client, Clarové Systems is the **data controller** for the personal data you provide us. When we build or operate software on your behalf and your end-users interact with that software, Clarové Systems acts as a **data processor** under your instructions. In that case, you (the client) are the data controller and are responsible for your own privacy disclosures to your end-users. We will enter into a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with you to govern that relationship.

## 4. What Personal Data We Collect

We collect only the data that is necessary for the specific purpose it is needed for. The categories below reflect the data we collect in our role as a service provider and business operator.

### 4.1 Identity and Contact Data

Full name, business or company name, email address, phone number, and country of residence or business registration. This is collected when you enquire about our services, sign a service agreement, or onboard as a client.

### 4.2 Project and Requirements Data

Information you provide to enable us to build your software or deliver your requested service. This includes business requirements, product specifications, user journey descriptions, brand guidelines, design assets, written content, third-party API credentials or access tokens, and any other materials you share with us in the course of a project.

You are responsible for ensuring you have the legal right to share this information with us and that any personal data of third parties included in project materials is shared lawfully.

### 4.3 Contractual and Correspondence Data

Email correspondence, project briefs, signed service agreements, statements of work, change requests, support tickets, and any written communications exchanged between you and Clarové Systems.

We do **not** record or store telephone calls or video meetings. Any reference to a call or meeting is captured only if you or we follow up with a written summary via email, which is then retained as part of the project record.

### 4.4 Billing and Transaction Data

Invoicing details, billing name, company name and address for invoice purposes, and transaction reference records. We retain these for accounting and legal compliance purposes.

We do **not** collect, store, or process your payment card details. All card and direct debit transactions are handled entirely by third-party payment processors — currently Paystack, Flutterwave, and Stripe. Each of these processors operates under their own PCI-DSS compliant infrastructure and privacy policies. We receive only a transaction confirmation and reference number, never your raw card data.

### 4.5 Hosting and Managed Services Data

Where we manage hosting, domain registration, business email, or cloud infrastructure on your behalf, we may have access to data stored in those environments as required to perform our management duties. This data is treated as belonging to you. We access it only to provide the agreed service and do not use it for any other purpose.

### 4.6 Website Usage Data

Technical data collected automatically when you visit our website, including IP address, browser type and version, operating system, the pages you visit, time and date of visits, referring URL, and session duration. This is collected via server logs and analytics tools. It is used solely to maintain and improve our website.

### 4.7 End-User Data (Processor Role Only)

Where we develop software that your customers or end-users interact with, we may process their personal data strictly as your data processor, under your instructions, and subject to a DPA. The categories, volumes, and sensitivity of that data depend on the software we build and are your responsibility to define and disclose to your end-users.

## 13. Your Rights

Depending on where you are located, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in Section 15. We will respond within 30 days of receiving a verifiable request, or within any shorter period required by applicable law.

### 13.1 Nigerian Data Subjects (NDPA / NDPR)

- **Right to be informed** — to know how your data is collected and used
- **Right of access** — to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you
- **Right to rectification** — to have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected
- **Right to erasure** — to have your data deleted where it is no longer necessary or lawfully held
- **Right to restrict processing** — to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances
- **Right to data portability** — to receive your data in a portable format where technically feasible
- **Right to object** — to object to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing
- **Right to complain** — to lodge a complaint with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission at **ndpc.gov.ng**

### 13.2 UK Data Subjects (UK GDPR / DPA 2018)

You have the same rights as listed above. You also have the right to object to direct marketing at any time, which we will honour immediately without exception. You may also lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office at **ico.org.uk**.

### 13.3 EU / EEA Data Subjects (EU GDPR)

You have equivalent rights to those above. You may lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority in your EU member state of residence.

### 13.4 Canadian Data Subjects (PIPEDA)

- To know what personal information we hold about you and how it is used
- To access your personal information and request corrections
- To withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions, with reasonable notice
- To lodge a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada at **priv.gc.ca**

### 13.5 California and US Data Subjects (CCPA / CPRA and Other State Laws)

California residents have the right to:

- Know what personal information is collected, used, or disclosed
- Request deletion of personal information (subject to permitted exceptions)
- Correct inaccurate personal information
- Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information — **we do not sell or share personal data**
- Non-discrimination for exercising any of the above rights

Residents of other US states with enacted privacy laws (Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas, etc.) may hold equivalent rights under their respective state laws. We will honour verifiable requests from residents of any state with applicable privacy legislation.

## 14. Security

We implement technical and organisational measures appropriate to the risk level of the data we process. These include:

- Encrypted data transmission via TLS/HTTPS across all our systems and client-facing services
- Access controls and role-based permissions limiting who within our team can access project data
- Secure credential management practices (we do not store client passwords or card details in plain text or shared systems)

In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Nigeria Data Protection Commission within 72 hours of becoming aware of it, as required by the NDPA. Where applicable law also requires us to notify affected individuals directly, we will do so without undue delay.
